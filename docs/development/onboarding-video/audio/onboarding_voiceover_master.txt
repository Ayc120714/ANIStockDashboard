Welcome to ANI Stock Dashboard. This is your main command center. Start here each day to check the market pulse, then move into detailed analysis modules for better decisions.

Overview is your daily starting point. Use it to understand market breadth, index direction, and sector momentum before selecting any trading opportunities.

Long Term helps you view structural trend direction. Use this section to align your ideas with the broader market trend and avoid short-term noise.

Short Term is for tactical opportunity tracking. Use it to identify near-term momentum and swing setups while staying aligned with the larger trend.

Screens converts market context into actionable stock lists. Apply filters, scan candidates, and shortlist symbols that match your setup criteria.

Advisor gives guided interpretation to support your trade planning. Use it after screening to improve conviction, risk clarity, and execution quality.

Portfolio Manager is your risk and position control hub. Review open positions, monitor performance, and keep exposure balanced across your portfolio.

Alerts keeps your workflow proactive. Instead of watching everything manually, use alert triggers so you can act quickly when important levels are reached.

In Account Details, verify your profile information, update mobile number, and manage password security. Keep this section updated for smooth account access.

Broker Integration connects your account to broker APIs. Select your broker, save the setup, and validate the session to enable downstream trading workflows.

Onboarding is your learning hub. Watch the onboarding video and use course links to build stronger understanding of fundamentals, technicals, and execution flow.

Events is where updates and important sessions are published. Check this section regularly for announcements and learning opportunities.

Your daily routine is simple: Dashboard, Overview, opportunity modules, risk review, and continuous learning from Onboarding. Follow this process consistently to improve decision quality.
